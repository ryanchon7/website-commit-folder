Who am I?

I was born in Korea and grew up moving between different parts of the world — from the busy streets of Hong Kong to the open spaces of America and the calm rhythm of Japan. Those experiences shaped who I am today, giving me a mix of cultures, perspectives, and languages that help me adapt easily and connect with people from many backgrounds. Now, at 20 years old, I see myself as someone who enjoys exploring new ideas, sounds, and flavors, constantly learning from every place I’ve lived.

My Favorite Music Artists

One of my biggest passions is music. I’ve always been drawn to the emotion andl rhythm of rap and hip-hop — the way it tells real stories with energy and creativity. I listen to artists like Drake, Young Thug, Lil Tecca, Lil Uzi Vert, and JSSR, who all inspire me in different ways. Music gives me focus when I work and freedom when I need to relax; it’s something that connects my different experiences no matter where I am.

My Favorite Sports

Sports have also been a big part of my life. I love the strategy of baseball, the fast pace of basketball, the intensity of football, and the thrill of skiing down a mountain. Sports have taught me discipline, teamwork, and persistence — lessons that apply far beyond the field. Whether it’s competing or just watching a good game, I find it’s one of the best ways to challenge myself and stay grounded.

My Favorite Foods

And then there’s food — one of the most joyful ways I connect with culture. My favorite cuisines are Korean, Japanese, Mexican, and French, each representing a different part of my journey. I love the bold flavors of Korean dishes, the balance in Japanese food, the spice of Mexican meals, and the elegance of French cuisine. For me, food is not just about eating — it’s about memories, creativity, and the feeling of home wherever I go.
